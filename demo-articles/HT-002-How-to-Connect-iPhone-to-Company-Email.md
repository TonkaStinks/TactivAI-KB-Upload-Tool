# How to Connect iPhone to Company Email

## When to Use This Article
Use this guide to set up your company email on your iPhone or iPad using the Outlook app or native Mail app.

## Prerequisites
- iPhone running iOS 14 or later
- Your company email address and password
- Internet connection (WiFi or cellular)
- Multi-factor authentication already set up on your account

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Option A: Using Outlook App (Recommended)

#### Step 1: Download Outlook
1. Open the App Store on your iPhone
2. Search for "Microsoft Outlook"
3. Tap "Get" to download and install

#### Step 2: Add Your Account
1. Open the Outlook app
2. Tap "Add Account" or the + icon
3. Enter your company email address
4. Tap "Add Account"

#### Step 3: Authenticate
1. You'll be redirected to your company login page
2. Enter your password
3. Complete multi-factor authentication (approve on Authenticator app or enter code)

#### Step 4: Configure Settings
1. Choose whether to add another account or tap "Maybe Later"
2. Allow notifications if prompted
3. Your email will begin syncing

### Option B: Using Native Mail App

#### Step 1: Open Settings
1. Go to Settings > Mail > Accounts
2. Tap "Add Account"
3. Select "Microsoft Exchange"

#### Step 2: Enter Account Details
1. Email: Your full company email address
2. Description: "Work Email" (optional)
3. Tap "Next"

#### Step 3: Sign In
1. Tap "Sign In" when prompted
2. Enter your password on the Microsoft login page
3. Complete multi-factor authentication

#### Step 4: Choose What to Sync
Select what you want to sync:
- Mail (required)
- Contacts (recommended)
- Calendars (recommended)
- Reminders (optional)

Tap "Save"

## Common Issues

### Issue 1: Authentication Keeps Failing
**Symptoms:** Keeps asking for password, won't connect
**Solution:**
- Make sure you're using your email password, not a PIN
- Check that MFA is set up on your account
- Try removing the account and adding it again

### Issue 2: Email Not Syncing
**Symptoms:** Inbox empty or not updating
**Solution:**
- Pull down to refresh
- Check Settings > Mail > Accounts > Fetch New Data
- Ensure "Push" is enabled for your account

### Issue 3: Calendar Not Showing Events
**Symptoms:** Calendar appears empty
**Solution:**
- Open Calendar app > Calendars (bottom)
- Make sure your work calendar is checked/enabled
- Wait a few minutes for initial sync

## Escalation Triggers
- "Your organization does not allow this device" error
- Repeated MFA prompts that won't complete
- Account adds but no data syncs after 30 minutes

## Success Criteria
- New emails appear on your phone
- Can send emails from your company address
- Calendar shows your meetings
- Contacts sync if enabled
